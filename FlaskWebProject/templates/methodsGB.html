The Gradient Boosting Classification Model

Based on this confusion matrix comparing the predicted and actual values of the Gradient Booster Classification machine learning model, it is apparent that the model created is relatively accurate; when you take all true postive and true negative predictions and divide them by the total observations, you get about 0.77, which is pretty high.

The fairly high weighted average precision rate leads to a similar conclusion; about 77% of predictions ended up being correct, which makes a pretty strong case for this model's precision.



Furthermore, this model has a weighted average recall ratio of 0.77. This shows that the model has a 77% chance of correctly predicting positive or negative observations compared to the total number of actual positive or negative observations.

Lastly, when you look at the F1-Score, which is the weighted average of the precision and recall of the model, it is evident that the previous observations are further confirmed. With a weighted average F1-score of 0.77, it is safe to say that this model could be reliable for predicting diabetes than the Decision Tree Model.
