Diabetes Machine Learning Predictor

Project Summary:

Our project is based on data from the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases regarding potential predicting factors of diabetes among females at least 21 years old of Pima Indian heritage. The size of the dataset is just over 700, and records factors such as number of pregnancies, glucose, blood pressure, skin thickness, insulin, BMI, Diabetes Pedigree Function, and age among participants.

The purpose of this project is to create a machine learning tool that will predict whether someone has diabetes or not, depending on the factors mentioned above. Our team tested out the accuracy of four different Machine Learning Classification Models (Decision Tree, Gradient Boosting, KNeighbor, and Logistic Regression), decided that the Logistic Regression model was the most accurate when looking at the F1-Score, precision, accuracy, and recall.
